The regulatory frameworks for Mahua spirits and general liquor sales vary significantly across Odisha, Jharkhand, and Chhattisgarh. Odisha actively licenses Mahua flower storage, transportation, and country liquor production, with confirmed plans to introduce high-quality, flavored Mahua liquor in its 2025-26 excise policy [2, 3, 20, 25]. Jharkhand has recently overhauled its general retail and wholesale liquor licensing through a suite of 2025 rules, though specific recognition of Mahua as a traditional spirit remains at the proposal stage [11, 15, 16]. Chhattisgarh regulates the supply of country liquor and recently amended its dry-day policies, but is currently only considering granting Mahua official "heritage" status [6, 14, 22].

Here is the detailed breakdown of the official government orders and regulations for each state based on current excise policies.

## Odisha: Active Licensing and Future Integration

Odisha maintains a structured, active regulatory framework for the collection, storage, and distillation of Mahua flowers, with upcoming policies aimed at modernizing the spirit.

* **Licensing and Fees:** The state government actively issues licenses for the sale and storage of Mahua flowers, such as a documented 2025 license renewal granted to individual operators [25]. The official utilization fee for Mahua flowers is set at Rs.275 per quintal [8].
* **Transportation and Production:** The transportation and collection of Mahua flowers across different parts of the state require a No Objection Certificate (NOC) endorsed by the State Excise Department [2]. The state also permits the production of Mahua-based country liquor under specific capacity licenses; for example, pre-feasibility reports indicate operators being licensed to produce 810 liters per day of Mahua flower-based spirit [20].
* **Excise Policy 2025-26:** To curb the illicit liquor trade, the Odisha government's upcoming Excise Policy 2025-26 includes initiatives to promote millet-based liquor alongside plans to prepare high-quality and flavored liquor derived from Mahua [3, 4, 17].
* **Retail Operations:** General excise regulations dictate that once a liquor shop is settled and begins functioning, it cannot ordinarily be shifted to a new location [8].

## Jharkhand: Overhauled Liquor Rules and Mahua Proposals

Jharkhand governs its alcohol sector through newly implemented 2025 regulations, though Mahua currently lacks a distinct, formalized legal category separate from general country liquor.

* **2025 Retail and Wholesale Rules:** The Jharkhand government recently notified the implementation of the "Jharkhand Utpaad (Licensing and Operation of Retail Liquor Shops) Rules, 2025," which governs retail operations [15, 18]. Wholesale operations are now regulated under the newly issued Jharkhand Liquor Storage and Wholesale Sale (Amendment) Rules, 2025 [11].
* **Licensing Allotment:** Under the Jharkhand Excise (Settlement and Operation of Shops for Retail Sale of Liquor) Rules, 2025, the state has invited applications to allot liquor shop licenses for a five-year period [24, 26]. Previously, the state had passed orders approving the retail sale of liquor exclusively through a state-owned beverage corporation [19].
* **Status of Mahua:** While Mahua from Jharkhand is viewed as a strong candidate for a Geographical Indication (GI) tag, it is not yet officially branded by the state [7]. State officials have proposed recognizing Mahua as a traditional liquor—similar to the legal status of Feni in Goa—and have suggested establishing government-run distillation stills in Adivasi areas, but these remain suggestions rather than enacted laws [16].

## Chhattisgarh: Country Liquor Supply and Heritage Considerations

Chhattisgarh regulates the broader country liquor market through state tenders and operational amendments, but has taken a cautious approach to officially rebranding Mahua.

* **Country Liquor Tenders:** The state manages its country liquor supply through official government notices. Most recently, on February 26, 2026, the Government of Chhattisgarh issued a notice inviting offers for the registration and supply of country liquor [22].
* **Operational Hours and Dry Days:** The Chhattisgarh government recently introduced a significant change to its excise policy by reducing the number of mandatory dry days. Liquor shops in the state are now permitted to operate on Holi, Muharram, and Gandhi Nirvana Day [14, 21].
* **Status of Mahua:** Despite the central Indian government's broader initiative to champion and rebrand Mahua liquor, Chhattisgarh has not yet capitalized on the spirit's commercial potential [5]. The state government is currently "mulling" over the possibility of declaring Mahua a "heritage drink" to mirror the legal framework established by neighboring Madhya Pradesh, but officials remain cautious about officially implementing this status [6].

## Summary

* **Odisha** actively regulates Mahua through Rs.275/quintal utilization fees, mandatory NOCs for transport, and specific production licenses, with the 2025-26 Excise Policy planning to introduce high-quality, flavored Mahua liquor.
* **Jharkhand** regulates alcohol through its newly implemented 2025 retail and wholesale licensing rules, offering 5-year shop licenses, while official recognition of Mahua as a "traditional liquor" remains a proposed concept.
* **Chhattisgarh** recently amended its excise policy to allow liquor sales on former dry days (Holi, Muharram) and manages country liquor via state tenders, but is currently only considering granting Mahua official "heritage" status.
