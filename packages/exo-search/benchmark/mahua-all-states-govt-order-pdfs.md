## Madhya Pradesh

A downloadable PDF of the Madhya Pradesh Heritage Liquor Rules 2023 is available through document-hosting platforms like Scribd, while a government notification PDF detailing specific excise exemptions for heritage liquor is hosted via Avantis. Regarding the 2025-2026 excise policy, the Madhya Pradesh government did not introduce any new amendments for Mahua liquor; the official policy explicitly stated that the regulations for heritage liquor would remain unchanged from the 2024-2025 framework.

Below is a detailed breakdown of where to access these regulatory documents, the specific rules governing Mahua liquor, and the subsequent policy shifts implemented through the 2026-2027 fiscal year.

### Accessing the Madhya Pradesh Heritage Liquor Rules 2023

The official framework governing traditional Mahua-based alcohol is formally titled "The Madhya Pradesh Heritage Liquor Rules, 2023" [2]. These rules apply to the manufacture, export, and transport of heritage liquor within the state [2].

To access the official documents and notifications in PDF format:
*   **Full Rules Document:** A complete, free-to-download PDF of the Madhya Pradesh Heritage Liquor Rules 2023 is available on Scribd [3].
*   **Excise Exemption Notification:** A specific government notification PDF, which outlines the exemption of Heritage Liquor from excise duty and export fees under the Madhya Pradesh Foreign Liquor Rules, is hosted on the Avantis legal update database [6].
*   **Government Portals:** While the official Madhya Pradesh government press website (govtpressmp.nic.in) hosts state gazette notifications, specific direct links to the 2026 heritage documents may currently be missing or require manual database searches [11].

### Implementation and Amendments
The Madhya Pradesh Heritage Liquor Rules 2023 officially came into effect on April 19, 2023 [4][5]. Later that same year, the Government of Madhya Pradesh issued a notification detailing amendments to these rules, which became effective on November 24, 2023 [7][8].

### The 2025-2026 Excise Policy Regarding Mahua Liquor

If you are looking for specific amendments to Mahua liquor in the 2025-2026 excise policy, there are none. The Madhya Pradesh Excise Policy for 2025-2026 maintained the status quo for traditional brewers.

The official release for the 2025-2026 Heritage Liquor and Wine Production Policy mandated that the existing system and policies for heritage liquor remain entirely unchanged from the previous 2024-2025 fiscal year [1]. Furthermore, the broader 2025-2026 policy dictated that general liquor prices would remain static, with no increase in the Ex-Distillery Price [23].

### The Socio-Economic Impact on Traditional Brewers

Liquor distilled from Mahua flowers was officially granted the prestigious status of "heritage liquor" in Madhya Pradesh four years prior to the current regulatory framework [13][14]. However, the implementation of these formalized rules has created significant friction for traditional tribal brewers.

Under the new excise laws, traditional liquor brewers face criminalization if they operate outside the formalized system, as Mahua liquor must now be sold strictly with a separate, official permit [10]. Despite the government's initial push, the heritage Mahua liquor initiative has struggled commercially. The formalized drink failed to find a substantial consumer base, which eventually led to a halt in its production and turned the initiative sour for the local tribal populations who traditionally relied on it [14][17].

### Current State: The 2026-2027 Excise Policy Overhaul

To address the stalled production and commercial failures of Mahua liquor, the Madhya Pradesh Cabinet, chaired by Chief Minister Mohan Yadav, approved a heavily revised excise policy for the 2026-2027 fiscal year [18]. The new retail liquor shop arrangements under this policy will be active from April 2026 through March 31, 2027 [16].

#### Mahua Promotion and the "Booze Barter Plan"
The 2026-2027 policy introduces sweeping reforms specifically designed to facilitate the sale, production, and export of Mahua-based liquor [9][18]. To give a renewed push to the state's heritage liquor brand, the Madhya Pradesh excise department proposed a unique "booze barter plan" aimed at promoting Mahua alongside other select liquor brands [12][15].

#### Broader 2026-2027 Market Regulations
Beyond heritage liquor, the 2026-2027 excise policy implements strict controls on the broader alcohol market, prioritizing digital transparency [19]:
*   **Price Surges:** The policy introduces a 20% surge in general liquor prices. For consumers, this translates to a Rs.50 increase on a standard Rs.1300 bottle of liquor [19][22].
*   **Ban on New Shops:** The state has placed a strict ban on the opening of any new liquor shops. Furthermore, existing shop premises cannot be expanded, relocated, or subjected to standard renewals [19][20][21].
*   **Holy City Prohibition:** A complete ban on liquor sales remains strictly enforced across 19 designated holy cities and religious sites declared in 2025, which includes Ujjain, Omkareshwar, and Maihar [22].

## Odisha

Official notifications and PDF documents for the Odisha Excise Policy 2025-26 can be accessed through the State Excise Directorate portal (stateexcise.odisha.gov.in) [8], the Odisha State Beverages Corporation (OSBC) website [2][13], and state RTI portals [10]. Specific sections of the 2025-26 policy, such as the "Odisha Mohua Flower Sale License 2025" and "Chapter XIV" regarding retail liquor vends, have also been published and outline strict operational guidelines for the upcoming fiscal year [4][26].

The 2025-26 excise policy introduces significant changes to Mahua-based liquor production, standardizes licensing dates, and freezes the expansion of new liquor shops across the state.

### Mahua Flower Country Liquor Regulations
Out-Still units and the products sold through Out-Still shops are officially categorized by the state as Mahua-based Out Still Country Liquor [1]. For the 2025-26 financial year, the state has outlined specific regulatory parameters for the sale, storage, and possession of Mahua flowers and liquor:

*   **License Validity:** The Odisha Mohua Flower Sale License for the upcoming cycle is valid from April 1, 2025, to March 31, 2026 [4].
*   **Storage and Inventory Limits:** Licensees are subject to strict conditions ensuring that their unsold balance of Mahua flowers does not exceed specific kilogram limits, and they must adhere to designated storage regulations [4].
*   **Minimum Guaranteed Quantity (MGQ):** For Out Still shops that are settled through the state's lottery system, the MGQ is established at 1.5 quintals of Mahua Flower per Rs.1,000 of consideration [5].
*   **Possession Caps:** Under the Odisha Excise Act, individuals are legally permitted to possess or sell outstill liquor manufactured from Mahua flowers up to a maximum limit of 10 liters [12].

### Out Still Shop Licensing Rules for 2025-26
The licensing framework for Out Still shops has been updated for the 2025-26 period, following a previous extension where shops in 22 excise districts were renewed from August 1, 2024, to March 31, 2025 [2].

*   **Auction and Tender Process:** Chapter XIV of the 2025-26 Excise Policy specifically governs the regulation and operation of "left out" retail liquor vends during the auction-cum-tender process for the financial year [26].
*   **Freeze on New Shops:** Excise Minister Prithiviraj Harichandan announced that no new liquor shops will be opened in Odisha [27].
*   **Rural Restrictions:** The state continues its restriction on granting new 'ON' shop licenses in rural areas, a rule that allows exceptions only for three-star and above hotel establishments [14].
*   **Pricing Controls:** The 2025-26 policy explicitly states that no increase in the Excise Duty Price (EDP) will be granted during the policy year [20].

### New Product Initiatives: Flavored Mahua and Millet Liquor
A major focus of the 2025-26 policy is the modernization of traditional tribal beverages to curb illicit liquor trade and generate economic benefits for local farmers [6][9].

*   **Flavored Mahua Liquor:** The Odisha government is developing high-quality, flavored liquor derived from the local Mahua flower [6][30].
*   **Millet-Based Breweries:** The 2025-26 policy officially promotes the production of millet-based liquor and the establishment of millet breweries [16][17][18].

## Chhattisgarh

The official notifications and PDF documents for the Chhattisgarh Excise Policy are hosted on the state's official excise portal at **excise.cg.nic.in** [1, 5]. However, there are no specific notifications in the current records explicitly detailing "Mahua" or "heritage liquor" for Chhattisgarh's 2025-2026 policy; notably, recent "Heritage Liquor Rules" were implemented by the neighboring state of Madhya Pradesh, not Chhattisgarh [18].

### Official Website & PDF Access
*   **Excise Rules Section:** Hosts the foundational regulations, including the "Country Liquor Spirit Rules" and the "Foreign Liquor Rules 1996" [12].
*   **Notices Section:** Rate offers, policy amendments, and supply registrations are published under the notices page (`excise.cg.nic.in/notice`) [5].

### Country Liquor Regulations (2025-2026)
*   **Rule Amendments:** The state issued official amendments to the Chhattisgarh Country Spirit Rules, 1995. These notifications were published on March 21, 2025, and February 5, 2026 [1, 15].
*   **Supply and Registration:** On February 26, 2026, the government issued a formal notice inviting offers for the registration and supply of country liquor across the state [8].
*   **Excise Duties:** Under the updated Chhattisgarh Excise Act, 1915, the excise duty on country liquor is set at 50%, calculated based on the ex-factory price [10].

### Key 2025-2026 Excise Policy Updates
*   **Foreign Liquor Price Cuts:** The government scrapped the 9.5% additional excise duty previously levied on foreign liquor [2].
*   **Low-Alcohol Beverages:** The updated policy allows licensees to sell wine and RTD low-alcoholic beverages with up to 10% ABV [17].
*   **Operational Days:** The state altered its policy to permit liquor shops to remain open on Holi and Muharram [13].

## Jharkhand

In 2025, the Jharkhand Excise Department implemented a comprehensive overhaul of its retail liquor sales, transitioning from state-run operations to a privatized model under the Jharkhand Excise (Settlement and Operation of Shops for Retail Sale of Liquor) Rules, 2025 [1][3]. While the provided government notifications do not explicitly mention "Mahua" liquor by name, they establish strict new guidelines for country liquor, spiced country liquor, and retail operations in tribal tourist zones [11][15].

### Privatization and the 2025 Retail Rules
The state government officially ceased selling alcohol through state-run outlets, with private vendors taking over retail operations starting March 1, 2025 [14]. The overarching settlement policy governs the state's liquor retail sector for a five-year period until March 31, 2030 [3].

### Regulations on Country Liquor and Traditional Spirits
*   **Tribal Tourist Zones:** Liquor outlets are permitted in districts and panchayats where the tribal population exceeds 50% [11].
*   **Packaging and Labeling:** Country liquor and spiced country liquor must be sold exclusively in approved bottles or cans featuring labels that display specific legends mandated by the state [15].
*   **Possession Limits:** 72 bulk litres (Bls) for country liquor and foreign spirit [26].

## Goa

The latest 2024-2026 updates to the Goa Excise Duty Act, 1964, introduce significant modernization efforts, including the Goa Excise Duty (Amendment) Rules, 2026, which mandate tamper-proof QR labels on all liquor bottles, and the Goa Jan Vishwas (Laws Amendment) Act, 2026, which decriminalizes minor offenses under Section 35 of the Act. Regarding Mahua spirit and IMFL, the government continues to classify Indian-manufactured Mahua as IMFL.

### Classification and Regulation of Mahua Spirit and IMFL
Under the regulatory framework established by the Department of Finance in Goa, "Mahua spirit, Mahua Liqueur and Agave beer" manufactured in India are officially declared and classified as "Indian Made Foreign liquor" (IMFL) [1].

### Goa Excise Duty (Amendment) Rules, 2026
Key implementations:
*   **Tamper-Proof QR Labeling:** High-security, tamper-proof excise labels on all liquor bottles [9][29].
*   **Business Verification:** New mandatory protocols for verification of Principal Place of Business [3].

### The Goa Jan Vishwas (Laws Amendment) Act, 2026
Amends Section 35 of the Goa Excise Duty Act, 1964, to decriminalize and rationalize minor excise offenses [7][27].

### March 2026 Government Orders on IMFL Transportation
*   **Premises Closures:** Mandatory closure of all licensed liquor-selling premises on March 7, 8, and 9, 2026 [15].
*   **Possession Limits:** Maximum three quart bottles of IMFL (including Mahua spirit) or six 650ml bottles of beer [17][21][24].
